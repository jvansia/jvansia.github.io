Winter Veggetti

Warmup with me

Ever since my family got a Veggetti Spiral Vegetable Maker, we have been using it nonstop. It is a great investment because not only does it give you nicely cut pieces of whatever vegetable you choose to use, but it is SO much fun to use! If you’re in college, or have children, this tool is so handy and portable, and can be used to make fresh veggetti where ever you are.

Veggetti, for those of you who do not know, is a type of raw pasta usually made from zucchini. No cooking is needed to make this type of ‘pasta’. The way the zucchini comes out of the Veggetti maker looks like spaghetti hence the combination of the words veggie and spaghetti to make the word, “veggetti”.

This recipe calls for 1 zucchini in order to make 2 bowls of Winter Veggetti; the recipe is called Winter Veggetti because since it is so cold out, raw food is harder to eat, so I added some warm marinara sauce on top. 

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini
  • A jar of your favorite pasta sauce
  • Coconut oil (or any oil of your choosing)

Directions:

1. Making the veggetti :

Take your Veggetti Spiral Vegetable Cutter (this can be found at Kohls, Target, & Bed Bath & Beyond) and place your zucchini in the cutting side. Twists your wrists, and keep moving them in a spiral motion until there is no zucchini left.

2. Making the sauce:

This part is fairly simple. Take a pot, add a spoonful of coconut oil, and then add your sauce. Then let it sit for 5 minutes. Turn the fire off.

Pour this sauce on your veggetti, and ta-da, you are done!
