Say “Fheese!” (Fake ‘Ricotta Cheese’ Filling)

This place has a good vibe

My childhood was covered in cheese. I remember growing up, and making quick meals by taking vegetables, and melt cheese on top of it ( I thought I was being healthy!). Also, frozen pizzas were a must, and always stocked in my fridge. Growing up, cheese was my best friend. When transitioning into becoming a vegan, giving up cheese was the hardest part. But, then, because of my genius mother, I didn’t have to! This filling recipe which my mother crafted HAS to be shared. It tastes identical to that of ricotta cheese a person uses in ravioli, manicotti, etc. Even when my cousins come over to eat, and we make this recipe, they do not know (and they still don’t unitl now) that it isn’t real cheese! This past week, I used it to fill Manicotti.

Here is what you will need:

Ingredients:

  • Organic Extra Firm Tofu 15 ounces
  • A bag of ‘Mozerella’ Daiya Cheese  (Can be bought at Stop n Shop but if you are cooking for a large group or party, I would buy in bulk. Here is where you can get a bulk of Daiya Cheese:Daiya Mozzarella Style Shreds, 8 Ounce — 12 per case.Most of the times when I go to the grocery store, almost all the Daiya is gone, so Amazon is a safe bet!
  • Organic Baby Spinach 20 leaves
  • Olive Oil 1/4 tsp 
  • Dry oregano 
  • Sea Salt 1 tsp
  • Basil 2 leaves
  • Black Pepper

Directions:

For this recipe, an essential tool to have is a food processor–I used a Vitamix.

  1. Prepare the Tofu:Drain the water out of the tofu,  and place the tofu into your food processor. (The tofu is going to act as the ‘cheese’ in the way that it will offer the same kind of consistency or feeling when you eat it. ) Do not turn on the food processor yet.
  2. Add Taste to Tofu: The taste of the tofu comes after the other ingredients are added to the food processor. Add 20 leaves of baby spinach, 2 leaves of basil, a quarter of a tsp of olive oil, a tsp of sea salt, and a pinch of both black pepper and dry oregano.
  3. Mix! : When you are done adding all the ingredients into your food processor. Pulse _(turn the food processor off and on in cycles) _your mixture for a few minutes. Stop when the consistency you desire is achieved!
